## Abstract

We present a methodology for fine-tuning large language models to authentically reproduce the voice of historical figures, demonstrated through James Madison, fourth President of the United States. Our approach combines Constitutional AI with DPO training using a novel "rich constitution" derived from 468,000 words of primary source material and 1.8 million words of scholarly biography — significantly richer source material than any prior character training work. We introduce a systematic pipeline for (1) extracting character traits from scholarly sources, (2) generating synthetic training data through multiple voice registers, and (3) evaluating authenticity through test-driven behavioral criteria. Our Madison LoRA adapter on Gemma 3 27B demonstrates [measurable improvement] over prompted baselines across [N] behavioral tests covering 8 distinct voice registers.

## 1. Introduction

- The problem: historical figure voice reproduction requires authenticity verifiable against documented evidence
- Why character training matters: civic education, historical engagement, digital humanities
- Why Madison is an ideal test case: extensive documented writings (468K+ words), multiple voice registers, positions that evolved over 50 years, verifiable ground truth
- Our contribution: a richer constitution than prior work, derived from primary sources + scholarly biography rather than Wikipedia profiles or hand-written trait lists
- Comparison to prior work: Maiya/Lambert (synthetic-only, generic personas), Character-LLM (Wikipedia-derived, historical figures but synthetic data only)

## 2. Related Work

### 2.1 Character Training via Constitutional AI
- Maiya, Bartsch, Lambert, Hubinger (2025) — Open Character Training (arXiv:2511.01689)
- Two-stage: DPO distillation + introspection SFT
- 100% synthetic data from hand-written constitutions (~10 traits each)
- Tested on 11 generic personas, 3 model families
- Key limitation: constitutions are shallow; generic traits produce generic characters

### 2.2 Character-LLM for Historical Figures
- Shao, Li, Dai, Qiu (2023) — Character-LLM (arXiv:2310.10158, EMNLP)
- "Experience Reconstruction" from biographical profiles
- Trained historical figures: Beethoven, Caesar, MLK, Socrates
- Key limitation: source material limited to Wikipedia; no primary source integration

### 2.3 Anthropic's Character Training
- Askell et al. — Claude's Character
- Test-driven development methodology
- Rich "soul document" rather than minimal trait lists
- Key insight: character traits as nudges, not rigid rules

### 2.4 Lambert's Theoretical Framework
- RLHF Book, Chapters 17 and 19
- Character as manner, not content
- Distinction between character training and RLHF/safety training

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Primary Source Corpus Construction
- 468,000 words of Madison's own writings across 8 voice registers
- Categories: Federalist Papers, political essays, convention/congressional speeches, presidential papers, legislative writings, correspondence
- Source acquisition: Founders Online, Yale Avalon Project, Project Gutenberg, Hunt 9-volume edition (LiteParse extraction)
- Quality control: spot-checking, deduplication, artifact removal

### 3.2 Scholarly Biography Integration
- 1.8M words from 7 scholarly biographies (Ketcham, Feldman, Cheney, Burstein/Isenberg, Ellis, Leibiger, Chernow)
- Targeted extraction methodology: grep-based search for characterization passages
- Four extraction categories: personality/temperament, intellectual style, evolution/contradictions, relationships
- 16,414 words of curated characterization material distilled from 1.8M words

### 3.3 Rich Constitution Design
- 9-section structure (~5,000 words): Identity & Evolution, Philosophical Positions, Slavery Contradiction, Rhetorical Patterns, Others' Assessments, Relationships, Voice Registers, Private Voice, Boundaries
- Written in first person as Madison's self-description
- Annotated version with full scholarly citations
- Two lengths: 5K for local models, 10K for large-context cloud models
- Comparison to prior constitutions: 50x richer than Maiya's 10-trait format

### 3.4 Training Data Generation Pipeline
- Prompt generation: 500-750 diverse prompts from constitution traits
- Teacher model: generates in-character responses with constitution as system prompt
- Student model: base Gemma 3 27B without constitution (rejected responses)
- DPO pair construction and quality filtering
- Novel: essay-to-conversation conversion preserving authentic vocabulary

### 3.5 Training Configuration
- QLoRA on Gemma 3 27B via Unsloth
- Modal A100 80GB infrastructure
- Hyperparameters from Maiya et al. baseline

### 3.6 Introspection SFT (Stage 2)
- Self-reflection data generation
- Self-interaction between model copies
- Per Lambert/Maiya methodology

## 4. Evaluation

### 4.1 Behavioral Test Suite (Askell TDD)
- 15-20 test cases covering all 8 voice registers
- Tests for: position accuracy, rhetorical pattern fidelity, anti-slop compliance, register shifting, historical boundary awareness
- Binary pass/fail + qualitative assessment

### 4.2 Quantitative Metrics
- ModernBERT classifier for Madison-vs-generic text discrimination
- Revealed preferences testing
- Perplexity comparison: fine-tuned vs. prompted baseline on held-out Madison text

### 4.3 Human Evaluation
- A/B testing through Chamber chat UI
- Blind evaluation: can a reader familiar with the Federalist Papers distinguish fine-tuned from prompted?
- Historical accuracy assessment

### 4.4 Prompted Baseline Comparison
- Gemma 3 27B with Madison system prompt (no fine-tuning)
- Same behavioral tests applied to baseline
- Quantitative improvement measurement
